 <p style="color:darkslategray; font-size:30px;">
    Royal Enfield is an Indian multinational motorcycle manufacturing company <b>headquartered in Chennai,
    Tamil Nadu,India</b>.The company is the oldest global motorcycle brand in continuous production,and 
    operates manufacturing plants in Chennai in India.The <u>first Royal Enfield motorcycle was built in
    1901 by the Enfield Cycle Company of England</u>,which was responsible for the design and original
    production of the <i>Royal Enfield Bullet, the longest-lived motorcycle design in history</i>.
    Licensed from Royal Enfield by the indigenous Indian Madras Motors, the <small>company is now a 
    subsidiary of Eicher Motors Limited,</small>an Indian automaker.The company makes classic-looking
    motorcycles including the <strong>Royal Enfield Bullet, Classic 350, Meteor 350,Classic 500, 
    Interceptor 650, Continental</strong> and many more.Royal Enfield also make adventurous and 
    offroading motorcycles like Royal Enfield Himalayan. Their <mark>motorcycles are equipped with
    single-cylinder and twin-cylinder engines.</mark></p>

 <p style="color:darkgreen;font-size:30px;">
    After the <mark>Indian Independence Act 1947</mark> the new government looked for a suitable motorcycle 
    for its army to patrol the country's border. In <small>1952 the Royal Enfield Bullet was chosen
    as the most suitable bike</small> for the job. In <b>1954, the government ordered 800 in units of the
    350 cc model</b>.In <i>1955, the Redditch company partnered with Madras Motors in India</i> to form 
    'Enfield India' to assemble,under licence, the 350 cc Royal Enfield Bullet motorcycle in Madras 
    (now called Chennai). The tooling was sold to Enfield India so that they could manufacture components.
    By 1962, all components were made in India. The <strong>Indian Enfield uses the 1960 engine</strong>
    (with metric bearing sizes), <u>Royal Enfield still makes an essentially similar bike in the 350 cc 
    and 500 cc models</u>, along with several different models for different market segments.</p>
